Who am I?

As You may have figured already, my name is Jesper Ebbesson. I'm 20 years of age, and am currently residing in Kalmar, Sweden. I'd like to call myself a "hobby artist", since I mainly just do it because I find it interesting and relaxing. The artist in me hasn't been active for quite a while, but looking back at my work, I realized how much I miss it. There's several reasons that I haven't been active, but one of them is the fact that I'm studying. I'm currently studying Web Development at the Linnaeus University in Kalmar, and have prior to that studied german.

I developed my interest for art, or to be more specific, digital art, a few years ago. It all started on a gaming forum. I saw those so called "forum signatures", which are, not going too deep into it, photo manipulations on a smaller scale. I immedeatly got inspired and thought, "Hey! I wanna be able to do that.". I got my hands on Photoshop and tried to make what I've seen, that's how it all started. I turned out that I had some kind of talent for it, at least some people thought so and encouraged me, for which they should have thanks since I wouldn't stand where I stand today without that.

After a while at gaming forum, I decided to move on. I found a site called Gfxresource.com, where I decided to become a part of the community. Said and done. The people there were very friendly, and I learnt a lot from them. I spent a lot of time on that site, and got accepted as a member of the staff. As a member of the staff I could finally return what the community had given to me. From that experience I have to say that you do learn a lot from helping and tutoring others. I continued learning even as a part of the staff.

As my repertoire of styles and my amount of work grew bigger, I decided that I wanted to make a blog to keep my work and updates on. That is the moment I got interested in coding. I couldn't achieve what I wanted with pre-made sites, so I started learning HTML and CSS. This was all durng the time I studied german, which I today speak fluently, and as my studies demanded more energy, my art and coding gained less time, and eventually I had to stop. I finally decided to start again as I looked back and realized how much I miss it, and this portfolio is my way of saying that I'm back.

I'm leaving You with these word from an old mentor of mine, "Mojo is a focused state of awareness that one enters to access their greatest creativity". So I'll simply say, LET DA MOJO LOOSE!
